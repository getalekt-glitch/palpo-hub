Questions
What is this, and why does it exist?

Not a teaching project, not a service ad. A practicing massage therapist from Minsk, over 20 years in. He writes about the body the way his hands see it: what he notices, what surprises him, what still remains unclear even after thousands of sessions. No recipes. No promises.

Do you give health advice here?

No. I'm not a doctor and don't claim to be. These are a practitioner's observations, not medical advice. If something hurts — see a doctor. This is something else: how the body behaves, what it says when hands listen to it.

Can I book a session with you?

Yes — studio and house calls. Studio is just outside the city, quiet, parking available, you can close the door and leave daily life outside. House calls — if the trip is too much and you'd rather fall asleep in your own bed right after the session. Details — direct message: @aleksei_massage_minsk.

Do you only work in Minsk?

Studio and house calls — Minsk only. Telegram and Pinterest — no geography. People read from many countries, and that's fine: observations about the body don't know borders.

How long have you been doing this?

Over 20 years. Came into the profession at 40, while building a house and running other things on the side. Worked hard — up to ten sessions a day. Still at it. Many who started around the same time aren't anymore.

What is "The Shelf"?

A section about the objects I work with: oils, tools, textiles. Not advertising — observations on how a specific thing changes the work. I only write about what I actually use.

Why do you use Pinterest?

Because it's search, not a feed. Someone looks up "what is shoulder tension" or "what a massage studio in Minsk looks like" — and ends up here. Pinterest moves slowly, and it lasts. A pin published a year ago still works today.
